Our History

Journey through Bangladesh's path to independence from 1952 to 1971

February 21, 1952

Language Movement Martyrs

Students and activists sacrificed their lives demanding recognition of Bengali as a state language of Pakistan. This pivotal moment ignited the Bengali nationalism that would eventually lead to independence.

Language Rights Student Movement Cultural Identity
February 5, 1966

Six Point Movement

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman presents the Six Point Program demanding autonomy for East Pakistan. This becomes the charter of Bengali freedom and earns him the title "Bangabandhu" (Friend of Bengal).

Political Movement Autonomy Bangabandhu
January-February 1969

Mass Uprising

A massive popular uprising against the Ayub regime forces the government to release Sheikh Mujibur Rahman and withdraw the Agartala Conspiracy Case. The movement strengthens demands for autonomy.

Mass Movement Student Protests Political Change
December 7, 1970

Historic Election Victory

Awami League wins a landslide victory in Pakistan's general election, securing 160 out of 162 seats in East Pakistan and an absolute majority in the National Assembly. This mandate is later denied by West Pakistan.

Democratic Victory Awami League Political Mandate
March 7, 1971

Historic Speech at Ramna Race Course

Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman delivers his iconic speech before millions, declaring "This time the struggle is for our freedom, this time the struggle is for our independence." This speech is now recognized by UNESCO as part of the Memory of the World.

Historic Speech UNESCO Heritage Independence Call
March 25, 1971

Operation Searchlight

Pakistani military launches a brutal crackdown on Bengali civilians, intellectuals, and political activists. This genocidal campaign marks the beginning of the nine-month Liberation War.

Military Crackdown Genocide War Begins
March 26, 1971

Declaration of Independence

Following his arrest, Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's declaration of independence is broadcast. Major Zia-ur-Rahman announces the independence on behalf of Bangabandhu from Chittagong, marking the formal beginning of Bangladesh as an independent nation.

Independence Declaration National Birth Broadcasting History
April 17, 1971

Mujibnagar Government

The provisional government of Bangladesh is formed at Mujibnagar (formerly Baidyanathtala) with Syed Nazrul Islam as Acting President and Tajuddin Ahmad as Prime Minister, providing political legitimacy to the liberation struggle.

Provisional Government Political Structure International Recognition
December 3, 1971

India Joins the War

Following Pakistani air strikes on Indian airfields, India formally enters the war in support of Bangladesh. The Mukti Bahini and Indian forces launch coordinated operations against Pakistani military positions.

International Support Military Alliance Joint Operations
December 14, 1971

Martyred Intellectuals Day

Pakistani forces and their local collaborators systematically kill prominent Bengali intellectuals, professors, doctors, engineers, and writers in a final attempt to cripple the emerging nation's intellectual capacity.

Intellectual Genocide Cultural Destruction Martyrdom
December 16, 1971

Victory Day - Birth of Bangladesh

Lieutenant General Amir Abdullah Khan Niazi signs the Instrument of Surrender at Ramna Race Course, Dhaka. With this surrender of 93,000 Pakistani troops, the nine-month Liberation War ends and Bangladesh emerges as an independent nation.

Victory Independence National Birth
January 10, 1972

Bangabandhu's Homecoming

Sheikh Mujibur Rahman returns to independent Bangladesh after his release from Pakistani prison. Millions gather to welcome the Father of the Nation home to lead the newly liberated country toward reconstruction and development.

Homecoming Father of Nation New Beginning
