About the Museum

Preserving History, Inspiring Future

The Liberation War Museum stands as Bangladesh's premier institution dedicated to documenting and preserving the history of our nation's struggle for independence. Established in 1996, the museum serves as a beacon of remembrance and education.

Our mission extends beyond preservation - we aim to inspire current and future generations with the stories of courage, sacrifice, and determination that shaped modern Bangladesh. Through our comprehensive collections and educational programs, we ensure that the legacy of 1971 continues to resonate.

Featured Collections

Path to Independence

A comprehensive journey through Bangladesh's struggle for freedom, from the Language Movement of 1952 to the final victory in December 1971. Features original documents, photographs, and personal accounts.

Explore Collection

Heroes and Martyrs

Honoring the brave souls who sacrificed everything for our freedom. This collection includes personal belongings, letters, and testimonies from freedom fighters and martyred intellectuals.

Explore Collection

Oral History Archive

Preserved testimonies from war veterans, survivors, and witnesses. These firsthand accounts provide invaluable insights into the human experience during the Liberation War.

Explore Collection

Photographic Heritage

A rare collection of photographs documenting the Liberation War, featuring images from Bangladeshi and international photographers who captured history as it unfolded.
